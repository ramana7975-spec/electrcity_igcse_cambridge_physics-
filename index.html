Electric Current (I)

Definition: The rate of flow of electric charge.

I = Q / t
I = Current (Amperes, A)
Q = Charge (Coulombs, C)
t = Time (seconds, s)

üõ°Ô∏è Safety Devices

1. Fuse

How it works: Thin wire that melts when current exceeds safe level, breaking the circuit.

Formula: Choose fuse rating slightly higher than normal operating current

I = P / V
I = Current needed (Amperes, A)
P = Power of appliance (Watts, W)
V = Voltage of supply (Volts, V)

Common ratings: 3A, 5A, 13A

2. Circuit Breaker

Advantages over fuses:

  • Works faster than fuses
  • Can be reset (no replacement needed)
  • Uses electromagnetic switch
  • More sensitive to current changes

3. Earth Wire

Protection mechanism:

  • Connects metal case to ground (0V)
  • If live wire touches case, large current flows to earth
  • This blows the fuse or trips circuit breaker immediately
  • Prevents user from getting electric shock

4. Double Insulation

For plastic-cased appliances:

  • Two layers of insulation material
  • No metal case = no need for earth wire
  • Symbol: Square within a square ‚ßà
  • Examples: hair dryers, power tools, phone chargers

üö® Emergency Procedures

‚ö° First Aid for Electric Shock

  1. DO NOT touch the person directly!
  2. Switch off the power supply immediately at the mains
  3. If you can't turn off power, use non-conductive object (wood, plastic) to move them away
  4. Call emergency services (999/112) immediately
  5. Check breathing and pulse
  6. If trained, perform CPR if necessary
  7. Keep person warm and calm until help arrives

üî• If Electrical Fire Occurs

  • Switch off power at mains if safe to do so
  • Use CO‚ÇÇ or powder fire extinguisher
  • NEVER use water on electrical fires!
  • Evacuate if fire spreads
  • Call fire brigade (999/112)
